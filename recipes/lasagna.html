Lasagna

Summary

Lasagna is a hearty, layered Italian dish that combines rich flavors and comforting textures. It typically features wide, flat pasta sheets alternated with savory layers of seasoned ground meat or vegetables, creamy béchamel or ricotta cheese, and tangy tomato sauce. Baked until bubbling and golden, the top layer is often crowned with melted mozzarella or Parmesan, adding a satisfying, slightly crispy finish. With its warm, indulgent taste and mouthwatering aroma, lasagna is a beloved classic that's perfect for family dinners, special occasions, or simply satisfying a serious comfort food craving.

List of Ingredients

  • Lasagna noodles
  • Ground beef or Italian sausage
  • Ricotta cheese
  • Mozzarella cheese
  • Parmesan cheese
  • Marinara or tomato sauce
  • Onion
  • Garlic
  • Olive oil
  • Salt
  • Black pepper
  • Fresh basil or Italian seasoning
  • Egg (optional, for ricotta mixture)

Steps to Make Lasagna

  1. Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C).
  2. Cook the lasagna noodles according to package instructions, then drain and set aside.
  3. In a large skillet, heat olive oil over medium heat. Add chopped onion and minced garlic; sauté until soft.
  4. Add ground beef or Italian sausage to the skillet and cook until browned. Season with salt, pepper, and Italian seasoning.
  5. Stir in marinara or tomato sauce and let it simmer for about 10 minutes. Remove from heat.
  6. In a mixing bowl, combine ricotta cheese with a beaten egg, a pinch of salt, pepper, and some chopped basil or Italian seasoning.
  7. In a baking dish, spread a thin layer of the meat sauce on the bottom.
  8. Layer lasagna noodles over the sauce.
  9. Spread a layer of the ricotta mixture over the noodles.
  10. Sprinkle a layer of mozzarella cheese on top of the ricotta.
  11. Repeat the layers (sauce, noodles, ricotta, mozzarella) until all ingredients are used, ending with a final layer of noodles and sauce.
  12. Top with shredded mozzarella and grated Parmesan cheese.
  13. Cover with foil and bake for 25 minutes.
  14. Remove the foil and bake for an additional 15–20 minutes until the top is golden and bubbly.
  15. Let the lasagna rest for about 10–15 minutes before serving.
