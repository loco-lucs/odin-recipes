Carne Asada Tacos

Mexican-style carne asada tacos are a flavorful street food favorite made with marinated and grilled beef—typically flank or skirt steak—sliced thin and served on warm corn tortillas. The meat is infused with a bold blend of citrus juice, garlic, chili powder, cumin, and fresh cilantro, then seared over high heat for a smoky, charred finish. Topped with chopped white onions, fresh cilantro, and a squeeze of lime, these tacos are often accompanied by spicy salsa and slices of creamy avocado or radishes for added crunch. Bursting with vibrant flavors, carne asada tacos are a beloved staple of Mexican cuisine, perfect for casual gatherings or a quick, satisfying meal.

List of Ingredients

  • 1 ½ lbs flank or skirt steak
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1/4 cup fresh lime juice (about 2 limes)
  • 1/4 cup orange juice
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 teaspoon chili powder
  • 1 teaspoon ground cumin
  • 1/2 teaspoon smoked paprika (optional)
  • Salt and black pepper, to taste
  • 1/4 cup chopped fresh cilantro (for marinade)
  • Corn tortillas (for serving)
  • 1/2 cup diced white onion (for topping)
  • 1/4 cup chopped fresh cilantro (for topping)
  • Lime wedges (for serving)
  • Salsa of choice (optional)
  • Sliced avocado or radishes (optional)

  1. In a bowl, whisk together lime juice, orange juice, olive oil, garlic, chili powder, cumin, smoked paprika (if using), salt, pepper, and chopped cilantro.
  2. Place the steak in a large resealable bag or shallow dish and pour the marinade over it. Seal or cover and refrigerate for at least 1 hour, preferably 4–6 hours for maximum flavor.
  3. Preheat a grill or grill pan over high heat. Remove the steak from the marinade and let excess drip off.
  4. Grill the steak for about 4–6 minutes per side, or until desired doneness. Let it rest for 5 minutes after cooking.
  5. Thinly slice the steak against the grain into bite-sized strips.
  6. Warm the corn tortillas on the grill or a dry skillet until soft and pliable.
  7. Assemble the tacos by placing sliced steak on the tortillas. Top with diced onion, chopped cilantro, and a squeeze of lime juice.
  8. Serve with optional toppings like salsa, sliced avocado, or radishes.
